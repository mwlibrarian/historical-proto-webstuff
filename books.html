The following is a sample of Books available through
the Upper Hudson Library System

Cookbooks for the Gluten Free Diet (key word search: Gluten Free)

  {A keyword search can also be done using the term: Gluten. However, be aware that the items will also include books designed to cook with gluten, which (from experience) may be frustrating upon receiving them.}

  • Washburn, Donna 125 best gluten-free recipes Ontario, Canada: Robert Rose, 2003
  • Cox, Michael Gluten-Free: more than 100 delicious recipes your family will love New York: Fireside Books, 2000
  • Reilly, Rebecca Gluten-free baking: more than 125 recipes for delectable sweet and savory baked goods… New York: Simon & Schuster, 2002
  • Hagman, Bette The gluten-free gourmet: living well without wheat New York: H. Holt, 1993
    • (Author has many books for gluten-free cooking.)
  • Sarros, Connie Wheat-free, gluten-free cookbook for kids and busy adults Chicago: Contemporary Books, 2004
    • (Includes improved bread recipes using new techniques.)
  • Berriedale-Johnson, Michelle Wheat-free, gluten-free: 200 delicious dishes to make eating a pleasure Chicago: Surrey Books, 2002
Support books for living/coping with Celaic Disease (keyword search: Celiac)
  • Green, Peter H. R., Jones, Rory Celiac Disease: A Hidden Epidemic Harpercollins, 2006
  • Kruszka, Bonnie J. Eating gluten-free with Emily: a story for children with Celiac disease Bethesda, MD: Woodbine House, 2004
    • (Highlights the struggles that children have at school and with attending social functions where food is served.)
  • Korn, Danna Kids with Celiac disease: a family guide to raising happy, healthy, gluten-free children Bethesda, MD: Woodbine House, 2001
    • (Written by a mother who has experience with raising a celiac kid from the age of 18months onward. This book was produced in cooperation with several healthcare professionals.)
