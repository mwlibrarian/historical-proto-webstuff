The following is a sample of available
Web Resources for more information on Celiac.

  • Celiac.com is a privately funded site that provides a “Celiac Disease and Gluten-free Diet Support Center” which includes important resources, support, and on-line resources that are helpful to those with Celiac disease or Gluten Intolerance in an effort to help them lead more comfortable and healthy lives.
  • Celiac Disease- Mayo Clinic.com is a resource for the newly diagnosed patient to explore the medical causes of their condition from a trusted source of medical information, the Mayo Clinic.
  • Celiac Kids.com is a website “by Danna Korn, founder of R.O.C.K. - Raising Our Celiac Kids, a support group for kids and their parents.”
  • Clinical Trials.gov:Celiac Disease is a website that puts patients, their families, and healthcare providers in touch with ongoing research in connection with Celiac disease; this site is sponsored by the National Institute of Health a government agency.
  • The Gluten-Free Restaurant Awareness Program (GFRAP) is a website that “facilitates the relationship between individuals with celiac disease and individually owned and operated restaurants that understand their dietary needs.” This site offers a directory, by location, of participating restaurants.
  • Journal of American Medical Association (JAMA) Patient page: Celiac disease is a page that gives a brief overview of Celiac disease for the patient; this site also offers links to recent JAMA articles relating to Celiac disease.
  • Medline Plus: Celiac Disease is a web portal for information about Celiac disease and related health conditions. This site provides many useful links to the new or veteran sufferer of Celiac disease, young or old.
